Timeline of Nizwa

6th century AD to 7th century AD

Between the 6th and 7th centuries AD, Oman had its capital in Nizwa.

9th century AD

The So'al mosque was built in Nizwa.

1670 AD

Imam Sultan bin Saif Al Ya'rubi built the Nizwa fort.

1950 AD

The tanuf villege was damaged and abandoned after the jebal akhdar war.

1970 AD

Sultan Qaboos bin Said overthrew his father and became the Sultan of Oman, leading to modernization and development of Nizwa.

1406 AD

In 1406 Bahla was ruled by Imam Makhzum ibn Al Fallah and was the capital of Oman and the seat of the ruling Nabhani dynasty.

500 AD

The Aflaj Irrigation Systems of Oman are ancient water channels from 500 AD.

1951 AD

Oman got independence from the britain.
